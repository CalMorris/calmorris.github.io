Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Influence of Culture on Values and Identity

I was born and spent my youth in South Africa. At the age of thirteen my family immigrated to New Zealand for my siblings and I to have a better life and more opportunities. My family are also of Dutch and British descent. A mixture of family culture and national culture has shaped me into becoming the person I am today.

The exposure has taught me to value work ethic and adaptability.

Immigrating can be tough because everything that was once familiar is now different, it requires you to change and adapt. Lifestyle, food and relationships are all similar but different.

Seek to understand and not be understood, is something that has stuck with me along the way. Living in a new country can be uncomfortable and it’s easy to get caught up in how things used to be done and resist change. Taking a moment to stop and understand another perspective has valuable because there is usually a reason why things are, the way they are.

In summary, I value work ethic, adaptability, curiosity and understanding.

Strengths and Weaknesses While Learning

My strengths are that have a curious mind and hard working. I am detail oriented and enjoy creating work that is of good quality. I am hard working and persevere when faced with a challenge.

My weaknesses are; I can become fixated when things aren’t working and as a result don’t slow down and solve the problem with a different perspective. I do better taking baby steps while learning but get caught up in the moment at times and want the outcome fast. I have learn't during the course that I also dont spend enough time reflecting and is something I am working on.

Overcoming Obstacles

In prior roles I have spent a bit of time completing personality profiles such as the Myers Briggs questionnaire and a few other similar isometric tests. I found them quite accurate and helped me understand some of my personal attributes which may help me overcome learning obstacles.

Perseverance is a quality which has helped me overcome many obstacles before. The ‘not yet’ attitude covered in the prep curriculum is something that will serve well with obstacles. Although perseverance is helpful, I also need to acknowledge when something is not working and change tact. Being mindful and self-aware will be important when facing obstacles.

I am also curious and like to understand how things work. Taking the time to learn and understand the obstacle will be beneficial. While this is a strength, I need to make sure I don’t get bogged down in details that aren’t relevant for the obstacle I am facing.

Ethical Decisions and Values

I was working in Canada as a performance analyst and coach. I was coaching of a team playing in the Oceania 7's rugby competition. We were travelling with a player who 8 months prior, sustained a concussion and was taking a long time to recover. She had been doing really well in training and our medical team decided that she was fit to travel to a tournament with one condition. Prior to playing, she needed to complete test which involved a battery of contact testing. If she completed the testing without symptoms during and after, she would be fit to play again. This is something the player had looked forward to for a very long time.

The player completed the tests without any symptoms and was cleared to play. The morning of the tournament the concussion symptoms returned and we were left to make a decision of whether she could play or not. We needed to decide if this was just a migraine or something related to her previous concussion.

After lengthy discussions between medical and coaching staff, we decided she could not play during the tournament. We had her long term health in mind. The player was very upset because was looking forward to the tournament. Not playing meant that she was not eligible for up-coming tournament selections for Canada.

Fortunately our conservative approach was the right decision as her symptoms continued for a further week. Allowing her to play could have some very serious consequences to her health.

The values in this decision were:

• Health and wellbeing come first. Always have the best intentions in mind, no matter how hard the path may be.

• Honesty and integrity is needed when faced with tough decisions.
