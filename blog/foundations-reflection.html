Foundations Reflections

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

  • I can be stubborn about the way I want to solve a problem and it’s good to change tact or take a break and come back to it later.

  • I don’t take enough time to reflect on what I have learn't after completing a project or overcoming an obstacle.

  • Regular failure is important for growth.

What are the role of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy is very important to helping someone. To better understand what they need help with, you need to place yourself in their shoes to understand the problem through a different lens. Self-awareness and self-regulation is needed when helping someone to ensure behaviour is appropriate. It is also essential while completing my own work to ensure I don’t become frustrated when faced with a bug or problem I can’t solve.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The value of making small things big through habits. Taking regular breaks using the Pomodoro technique has improved my ability to take on work with a clear mindset.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Understanding my values as it is not something I think about often.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

People are at the center of technology and it is an important skill to work alongside many different people. The better you work with people, the more enjoyable and productive it is.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, it has been enjoyable taking the time to think about the non-technical aspects of programming and apply it for better wellbeing and professional habits.
